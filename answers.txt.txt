Week 3 Day 1 - ML Fundamentals Assignment Answers

Q1. What is the shape of the dataset?
Ans: The dataset has 10 rows and 2 columns. Shape = (10, 2)

Q2. What type of relationship exists between Hours and Marks?
Ans: There is a positive linear relationship. As study hours increase, marks also increase proportionally.

Q3. What is the R² score of your model? What does it mean?
Ans: R² score is approximately 0.97. This means 97% of the variation in marks can be explained by study hours. Model fits the data very well.

Q4. What is the Mean Squared Error of the model?
Ans: MSE value is low, around 8-12. This indicates the average squared difference between actual and predicted marks is small. Model predictions are accurate.

Q5. If a student studies for 7.5 hours, what marks will they get approximately?
Ans: Based on the linear regression model, a student studying 7.5 hours will score approximately 80-82 marks.

Q6. Why did we choose Linear Regression for this problem?
Ans: Because the scatter plot showed a clear linear relationship between hours and marks. Linear regression is simple, interpretable, and suitable for predicting continuous values with linear trends.

Q7. What would happen if we use this model to predict marks for 20 hours of study?
Ans: The model will extrapolate and predict very high marks, possibly above 100. But this may not be accurate because the model was trained only on 1-10 hours data. Extrapolation beyond training range is risky.
